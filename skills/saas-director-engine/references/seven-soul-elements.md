# Seven Soul Elements

Use this checklist before finalizing a SaaSReels RenderSpec.

| Element        | Question                                                      | Failure Signal                                                 |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Hook           | Does scene 1 create curiosity, tension, or a desired outcome? | It starts with a logo, generic slogan, or product category     |
| Pain Reveal    | Does the script name the buyer's current friction?            | It says "streamline your workflow" without naming the workflow |
| Pain Amplify   | Does it show why the pain matters now?                        | The viewer can ignore the problem without consequence          |
| Solution Entry | Does the product arrive as the obvious next step?             | The product appears before the viewer understands why          |
| Core Benefit   | Does every feature become an outcome?                         | Copy lists capabilities with no buyer impact                   |
| Trust Proof    | Is there evidence, specificity, or a concrete demo moment?    | Claims feel unearned or generic                                |
| Clear CTA      | Is there exactly one next action?                             | The ending asks for multiple actions or none                   |

## Self-Audit Rubric

Score each item from 1 to 5.

- 5: Specific, visual, and tied to the brief or assets.
- 3: Present but generic.
- 1: Missing or misleading.

Revise any item below 4 before handing the spec to validation.

## Pacing Guidance

- For 12-20 second hook ads at 30fps, use 4-5 scenes.
- For 20-35 second landing-page summaries, use 5-7 scenes.
- For 30-50 second product demos or explainers, use 6-9 scenes.
- Avoid scenes longer than 150 frames unless the brief asks for a slower explainer.
- Choose duration after deciding whether the job is hook, summary, or demo. Do not force every SaaS video into one fixed length.
