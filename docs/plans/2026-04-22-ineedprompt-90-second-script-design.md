# Video Script Design: INeedPrompt 90-Second Power-User Explainer

This document captures the approved design for a 90-second English marketing video for `https://www.ineed-prompt.com/`.

## Overview

The goal is to create a polished SaaS explainer that does four jobs at once:
- introduce the website structure,
- demonstrate the core product workflow,
- sell the product to heavy AI prompt users,
- feel visually premium instead of like a screen recording.

This round is script-first only. Database and R2 concerns are intentionally out of scope.

## Audience And Conversion Goal

- **Primary audience:** heavy AI prompt users
- **Viewer profile:** people who already write prompts often and care about speed, consistency, and output quality
- **Primary pain point:** prompt writing is slowing down execution
- **Narration language:** English
- **Primary CTA:** `Start generating now`

## Recommended Creative Approach

Use a **Hybrid Power-User Story** approach.

This combines:
- the clarity of a traditional product explainer,
- the visual polish of a cinematic SaaS promo,
- a direct commercial narrative for advanced users.

Why this approach:
- a pure cinematic film would look good but under-explain the product,
- a pure walkthrough would explain the UI but feel too flat,
- the hybrid approach lets the story move from pain -> product -> proof -> conversion.

## Narrative Shape

The story should follow this flow:

1. **Hook**
   Hit the pain immediately: the user has ideas, but prompt writing speed is the bottleneck.
2. **Reframe**
   Position INeedPrompt as an accelerator from rough concept to professional prompt.
3. **Guided Product Tour**
   Use the website structure to introduce the interface and reduce learning friction.
4. **Proof Of Better Output**
   Show that the tool improves not only speed, but also prompt quality and stability.
5. **Commercial Layer**
   Introduce credits as a natural fit for power users with repeated generation needs.
6. **CTA**
   End on a single low-friction action: `Start generating now.`

## Visual Direction

Use a **light UI base with dark cinematic accents**.

- Preserve the existing INeedPrompt visual identity: clean white layout, blue-violet highlights, minimal UI.
- Shift into darker, glow-heavy, cinematic moments for major feature reveals and emotional peaks.
- Keep product UI as the hero. Atmosphere supports the interface instead of replacing it.

### Motion Language

- Fast, high-contrast opening for the hook
- Smooth guided camera movement for the product tour
- Clear cause-and-effect transitions for Generate and Supplement flows
- Floating UI cards and amplified typography for proof moments
- Minimal flashy transitions; prefer elegant wipes, pushes, glow reveals, and focus shifts
- Return to a clean, bright finish for the CTA

## Beat Plan

The approved structure is 8 beats across 90 seconds:

1. **Beat 1, 0-10s, Hook**
   Prompt writing is slowing down people who already know what they want to create.
2. **Beat 2, 10-20s, Reframe**
   INeedPrompt becomes the bridge from idea to production-ready prompt.
3. **Beat 3, 20-34s, Generator Intro**
   Introduce the main interface and the two-mode workflow.
4. **Beat 4, 34-48s, Generate Workflow**
   Show turning a raw concept into a richer, more structured prompt.
5. **Beat 5, 48-60s, Supplement Workflow**
   Show how advanced users can upgrade an already decent prompt instead of rewriting from scratch.
6. **Beat 6, 60-72s, Output Quality Proof**
   Use showcases and examples to prove the output gets stronger, not just faster.
7. **Beat 7, 72-82s, Commercial Layer**
   Position credits as the practical engine for frequent users.
8. **Beat 8, 82-90s, CTA**
   Land on brand, clarity, and action: `Start generating now.`

## Deliverables For This Script-First Pass

Create the following files in the target asset directory:

- `SCRIPT.md`
  English narration script with beat durations and voiceover copy
- `STORYBOARD.md`
  Beat-by-beat creative direction with on-screen action, motion, emphasis, and asset mapping
- `render-spec.draft.json`
  Draft SaaSReels RenderSpec created only after the script and storyboard are approved

## Asset Strategy

Use existing product assets as the primary evidence:

- `功能区域.png`
  Main feature and workflow demo asset
- `showcases.png`
  Showcase and output proof asset
- `案例区域.png`
  Secondary proof / results-support asset
- `price.png`
  Pricing and credits commercial beat asset
- `demo-video.mp4`
  Supportive motion reference or atmospheric bridge, not the main storytelling layer

### Stock Footage Policy

- Pexels footage may be used only as support
- Product visuals must lead the story
- No stock footage should replace the functional explanation of the product

## Success Criteria

- The script feels written for advanced prompt users, not beginners
- The website and product workflow are easy to understand without becoming a dry walkthrough
- The video contains a clear commercial argument for repeated usage
- The CTA feels earned by the story
- The visual plan supports a premium SaaS look while staying recognizably INeedPrompt

## Out Of Scope

- Database writes
- R2 uploads
- Final rendered MP4 delivery
- Production asset synchronization outside the local working folder
